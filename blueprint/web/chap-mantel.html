9 Application: Mantel’s theorem

As a worked application of the whole framework, we prove Mantel’s theorem — the Turán density of the triangle \(K_3\) is \(\tfrac 12\). The flag-algebra upper bound (a sum-of-squares certificate, Chapter 6) and the extremal lower bound meet in Theorem 120. (Source: LeanFlagAlgebras/MantelTheorem/.)

For every positive homomorphism \(\varphi \) with \(\varphi (K_3) = 0\) (a triangle-free limit), \(\varphi (K_2) \le \tfrac 12\).

Lemma 119 Complete-bipartite lower bound
✓

Lean declarations

  • MantelTheorem.extremal_density_K3_ge

For \(n \ge 2\), the maximal edge density of a triangle-free graph on \(n\) vertices is at least \(\tfrac 12\), witnessed by the balanced complete bipartite graph: \(\operatorname {ex}(n,K_3)/\binom {n}{2} \ge \tfrac 12\).
