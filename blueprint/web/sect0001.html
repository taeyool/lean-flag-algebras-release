1 Mantel’s theorem

The flag algebra \(\mathcal{A}^0\) of graphs is generated by isomorphism classes of small graphs, with a multiplication given by gluing on disjoint vertex sets and averaging. Working inside \(\mathcal{A}^0\), Mantel’s theorem becomes a single algebraic inequality certified by a sum of squares. This chapter is a proof-of-concept slice of the LeanFlagAlgebras library: the flag-algebra upper bound and the extremal lower bound are proved independently and meet in Theorem 7.

For a positive homomorphism \(\varphi \) (a graph limit), \(\varphi (K_2)\) is its edge density and \(\varphi (K_3)\) its triangle density.

Expanding the edge density onto three vertices,

\[ K_2 \; =\; \tfrac {1}{3}\, E_3 + \tfrac {2}{3}\, P_3 + K_3, \]

where \(E_3\), \(P_3\), \(K_3\) are the one-edge, two-edge (path), and triangle densities on three vertices.

Lemma 3 Square-positivity certificate
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • MantelTheorem.FlagAlgebra_2_1_0_0_minus_FlagAlgebra_2_1_0_1_square_downward

Unlabelling the square of the difference of the two rooted two-vertex flags \(O_2^{1}\) and \(K_2^{1}\) (rooted at one vertex) yields

\[ [\! [\, (O_2^{1} - K_2^{1})^2 \, ]\! ]_0 \; =\; O_3 - \tfrac {1}{3} E_3 - \tfrac {1}{3} P_3 + K_3 \; \ge \; 0 . \]

The left-hand side is a square, hence nonnegative in \(\mathcal{A}^0\); this is the sum-of-squares certificate driving the theorem.

Lean declarations

  • MantelTheorem.Mantel_theorem

In the flag algebra \(\mathcal{A}^0\),

\[ K_2 \; \le \; \tfrac {1}{2}\cdot 1 + K_3 . \]
Proof ▶

Substitute the expansion of \(K_2\) from Lemma 2 and add the sum-of-squares certificate of Lemma 3 together with the nonnegativity of the individual three-vertex densities. Collecting terms writes \(\tfrac {1}{2}\cdot 1 + K_3 - K_2\) as a nonnegative combination, which is exactly the claimed inequality.

Theorem 5 Mantel, homomorphism form

Lean declarations

  • MantelTheorem.Mantel_theorem'

For every positive homomorphism \(\varphi \) with \(\varphi (K_3) = 0\) (every triangle-free graph limit), the edge density satisfies \(\varphi (K_2) \le \tfrac {1}{2}\).

Proof ▶

Apply \(\varphi \) to the inequality of Theorem 4. As \(\varphi \) is a positive algebra homomorphism, \(\varphi (K_2) \le \tfrac {1}{2} + \varphi (K_3) = \tfrac {1}{2}\).

Lemma 6 Complete-bipartite lower bound
✓

Lean declarations

  • MantelTheorem.extremal_density_K3_ge

For \(n \ge 2\) the maximal edge density of a triangle-free graph on \(n\) vertices is at least \(\tfrac {1}{2}\), witnessed by the balanced complete bipartite graph:

\[ \frac{\operatorname {ex}(n, K_3)}{\binom {n}{2}} \; \ge \; \tfrac {1}{2} . \]

  • MantelTheorem.Turan_density_K3

The Turán density of \(K_3\) equals one half:

\[ \pi (K_3) \; =\; \tfrac {1}{2} . \]
Proof ▶

The homomorphism form (Theorem 5) gives the upper bound \(\pi (K_3) \le \tfrac {1}{2}\), while the complete-bipartite construction (Lemma 6) gives the matching lower bound \(\pi (K_3) \ge \tfrac {1}{2}\). The two bounds meet at \(\tfrac {1}{2}\).
