4 The flag algebra \(\mathcal{A}^\sigma \)

Formal real combinations of flags form a free module; a product defined through pair densities and a quotient by Razborov’s averaging relations turn it into a commutative \(\mathbb {R}\)-algebra, the flag algebra \(\mathcal{A}^\sigma \). (Source: FlagAlgebra/FlagAlgebra.lean.)

A size-tagged flag is a dependent pair \(\langle n, F\rangle \) of a vertex count \(n\) and a flag \(F\) on \(\mathrm{Fin}\, n\). These are the basis indices of the algebra.

Definition 29 Flag multiplication
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 28
  • Definition 21
  • Definition 36

Lean declarations

  • FlagAlgebras.flagMul
  • FlagAlgebras.flagMulWithSize

The product of two flags is expanded onto flags \(G\) of a target size \(\ell \), weighted by the pair density:

\[ F \cdot F' \; =\; \sum _{G} d_2(F,F';G)\, \cdot G, \]

taken at the minimal size \(\ell = |F| + |F'| - |\sigma |\) (\(\mathrm{flagMul}\)); the choice of \(\ell \) is irrelevant modulo the averaging relations.

\(\mathcal{A}^\sigma = \mathrm{FlagVector}\, \sigma / {\sim _v}\), flag vectors modulo the zero space. It carries a commutative \(\mathbb {R}\)-algebra structure (built below), with quotient map written \([\! [\, \cdot \, ]\! ]\).

Theorem 32 The zero space is an ideal
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 30
  • Definition 29

Lean declarations

  • FlagAlgebras.flagVector_mul_zeroSpace

Multiplying any flag vector by an averaging relation gives another element of the zero space. Hence multiplication descends to a well-defined operation on the quotient.

Proof ▶

Reduce to a basis vector times a generator \(\mathrm{zeroElement}\, F\, \ell \) and apply the chain rule (Theorem 24), which is exactly the statement that the product of the relation is again a telescoping sum of relations.

Theorem 33 Associativity modulo \(\sim _v\)
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 29
  • Definition 21
  • Theorem 42

Lean declarations

  • FlagAlgebras.flagVector_mul_assoc

\((f\cdot g)\cdot h \sim _v f\cdot (g\cdot h)\). Associativity fails on the nose on flag vectors but holds modulo the zero space, so it lifts to genuine associativity on \(\mathcal{A}^\sigma \).

Proof ▶

Both sides expand, via the triple-density identity (Theorem 42), to the same sum \(\sum _{G} d_3(F_1,F_2,F_3;G)\cdot G\).

Theorem 34 Product equals density expansion
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 31
  • Definition 29

Lean declarations

  • FlagAlgebras.basisVector_quot_mul_eq_flagMul_quot

In \(\mathcal{A}^\sigma \), \([\! [F]\! ]\cdot [\! [G]\! ] = [\! [\, \mathrm{flagMul}\, F\, G\, ]\! ]\): the ring product of two flags is the class of their density expansion.

Proof ▶

Unfold the multiplication on the quotient, which is defined by \(\mathrm{flagMul}\) and well-defined by Theorem 32.

Theorem 35 Same-size flags sum to one
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 31
  • Definition 28
  • Theorem 43

Lean declarations

  • FlagAlgebras.sum_flagWithSize_eq_one

For every \(\ell \ge |\sigma |\), \(\sum _{F : |F|=\ell } [\! [F]\! ] = 1\) in \(\mathcal{A}^\sigma \): the sum over all flags of a fixed size is the unit. This normalization is used pervasively in density proofs.

Proof ▶

Specialize the density-expansion identity (Theorem 43) to the empty flag, whose density in every size-\(\ell \) flag is \(1\).

Theorem 44 Nontriviality
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 31
  • Definition 30
  • Definition 28
  • Definition 21
  • Theorem 24

Lean declarations

  • FlagAlgebras.flagAlgebra_zero_ne_one

\(0 \ne 1\) in \(\mathcal{A}^\sigma \): the flag algebra is nontrivial (so its \(\mathrm{CommRing}\) structure is not the zero ring).

Proof ▶

If \(1\) lay in \(\mathrm{ZeroSpace}\, \sigma \) it would be a finite combination of averaging relations. But the linear functional \(\varphi (G') = d_1(G';F)\) (density into a fixed large flag \(F\)) sends every generator \(\mathrm{zeroElement}\, G\, \ell \) to \(0\) by the chain rule (Theorem 24), while \(\varphi (1) = 1\) — a contradiction.
