5 The unlabeling operator

Density bounds are ultimately statements about unlabeled graphs. The downward operator \([\! [\, \cdot \, ]\! ]_0 : \mathcal{A}^\sigma \to \mathcal{A}^\emptyset \) forgets a flag’s labelling by averaging over all ways to place the labels. (Source: FlagAlgebra/FlagOperators.lean; \(\emptyset \) denotes the empty type \(\varnothing _t\).)

Lemma 47 The bridge set, counted three ways

The set of pairs (vertex subset, injective label assignment) embedding a labeled flag into an unlabeled one is counted three ways: by isomorphism-count times labeled count; by summing labeled counts over labellings of the same graph; and, grouped by label-extension flag, over all relabellings.

Theorem 48 Counting identity
✓
#

Uses

  • Lemma 47
  • Definition 45
  • Definition 46
  • Definition 21
  • Definition 56

Lean declarations

  • FlagAlgebras.flagDensity_mul_downwardNormalizingFactor_eq_sum_labelExtensions

Equating the three counts yields

\[ d_1(\mathrm{unlabel}\, F;\, F')\cdot w(F) \; =\; \sum _{G \in \mathrm{ext}(F')} d_1(F;G)\cdot w(G), \]

where \(w\) is the normalizing factor and \(\mathrm{ext}(F')\) ranges over the relabellings of \(F'\). This is the algebraic engine of well-definedness.

Proof ▶

Combine the first and third counts of Lemma 47 and divide by the number of label injections.

Lemma 49 Downward respects the zero space
✓
#

Uses

  • Definition 30
  • Lemma 59

Lean declarations

  • FlagAlgebras.downwardFlagVector_zeroSpace

The unlabeling map sends the zero space of \(\sigma \) into the zero space of the empty type, so it descends to the quotient algebra.

Proof ▶

The zero space is spanned by the generating relations, each of which downward sends into the zero space of the empty type (Lemma 59).
